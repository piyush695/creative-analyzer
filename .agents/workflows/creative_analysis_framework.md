---
description: Creative Analysis Framework for analyzing ad creatives
---

This framework is a permanent instruction set. Activate these guidelines whenever analyzing ad creatives to ensure consistent, high-quality evaluations.

**1. Core Evaluation Frameworks**
* Deconstruct by Elements (Tagging): Break ads into smaller components—hook, visual style, body, offer, CTA, and audio. Tag these elements to identify which specific variable drives success.
* The 3-Second Hook Rule: Analyze the Thumbstop Ratio (3-second video plays ÷ impressions). If this is low, your opening 2-3 seconds need immediate work.
* Engagement vs. Action: Distinguish between engagement (likes, shares) and conversion (sales, clicks). An ad can have high engagement but poor conversion if the offer is weak.
* Identify Winning Patterns: Look for trends, such as: Does user-generated content (UGC) outperform polished brand video? Do problem-solution hooks out-perform product-feature hooks?
* Monitor Creative Fatigue: Track when an ad's CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) rises over time, indicating the audience is bored and the creative needs a refresh.

**2. Key Metrics for Creative Analysis**
* Thumbstop Rate/Hook Rate: Measures attention-grabbing capability (Target: 30–40%).
* Hold Rate/Engagement Rate: Measures if the ad holds interest (Target: 25%+).
* Click-Through Rate (CTR): Measures curiosity/intent to learn more (Target: >1.5% for Meta).
* Conversion Rate (CVR) & ROAS: The ultimate metrics for business impact.
* Average Watch Time: Identifies where viewers stop watching.

**3. Practical Steps for Analysis**
* Set Clear Goals: Define what success looks like (e.g., lower CPA, higher CTR) before looking at data.
* Centralize Data: Use tools to combine data from Meta, TikTok, and Google to see performance across channels.
* Apply "Inversion" Thinking: Instead of just asking "what worked?", ask "why did this ad fail?".
* Iterative Testing: Test one variable at a time (e.g., different headlines on the same visual) to pinpoint success.
* Analyze Competitors: Use AI tools to see what angles, visual styles, and offers competitors are scaling.

**4. Common Pitfalls to Avoid**
* Judging the Whole Ad: Do not treat an ad as a single piece. Good hooks are often buried inside low-performing ads.
* Ignoring Context: A high-performing ad in one market might not work in another, and an ad's success is relative to its spend.
* Too Much Data, No Action: Focus only on KPIs that align with your goals rather than drowning in data.
* Relying Solely on "Prettiness": Often, low-fidelity or "normalized" content (like UGC) performs better than high-budget production because it feels organic.
